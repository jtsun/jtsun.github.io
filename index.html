Personal Profile

Dr. Jiatao Sun has graduated from department of materials sciences and engineering, Shandong University in 2002 and 2005 with BA and MS respectively. Then he moved to Institute of Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences (IOP, CAS) and graduated in 2008 with a Ph. D majored in condensed matter physics under the supervisor Prof. Hong-Jun Gao. Afterwards, he continued his research career with postdoctoral positions. He has stayed in National University of Singapore and University of Utah (USA) for several years. In the end of 2013, he came back to Institute of Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences with a position of associate professor.

His research interest mainly lies at the theoretical investigation of quantum phenomena in low dimensional systems by using density functional theory (DFT) with model Hamiltonian. His past research experience includes the self-assembly mechanism of molecular adsorption on surfaces, design of electronic structures around surface and interface. These projects were done partially in collaboration with scanning tunneling microscopy experiments. Recently his main research interest is theoretical design of new quantum materials by using DFT calculations and Wannier functions including some physical questions such as the magnetism and quantum anomalous Hall effecton emerging at surface or interface, spintronics and valleytronics in two-dimensional materials etc. His main achievements include the discovery of spin-polarized state on pristine Si thin film, larger energy gap formed at the edge of Bi nanoribbon, change of Au(111) surface reconstruction etc. He has published about 40 papers in journals like PRL, Nano Lett., Adv. Mater., PRB, ACS Nano etc. The complete publication list is shown here ResearcherID.

Recent Publication

Magnetic Dirac Fermion and Chern Insulator on Pristine Silicon Surface

Phys Rev B 94, 035427(2016)

Emergence of ferromagnetism in nonmagnetic semiconductors is strongly desirable, especially in topological materials because of the possibility of achieving the quantum anomalous Hall effect. Based on first-principles calculations, we propose that for Si thin film grown on metal substrate, the pristine Si(111)-3¡Á3 surface with a spontaneous weak reconstruction has a strong tendency toward ferromagnetism and nontrivial topological properties, characterized by spin-polarized Dirac-fermion surface states. In contrast to conventional routes relying on introduction of alien charge carriers or specially patterned substrates, the spontaneous magnetic order and spin-orbit coupling on the pristine silicon surface together give rise to the quantized anomalous Hall effect with a finite Chern number C=-1. This work suggests opportunities in silicon-based spintronics and quantum computing free from alien dopants or proximity effects.

Spin-polarized valley Hall effect in ultrathin silicon nanomembrane via interlayer antiferromagnetic coupling

2D Materials 3,035026(2016)

Fundamental understanding of two-dimensional materials has spurred a surge in the search for topological quantum phase associated with the valley degree of freedom (VDOF). We discuss a spin-polarized version to the VDOF in which spin degeneracy is broken by the antiferromagnetic exchange coupling (LAFM) between opposite layers of the quasi-two-dimensional silicon nanomembrane (SiNM). Based on first principles calculations, we found that the LAFM state in SiNM can lead to metal-insulator transition (MIT). The broken degeneracy of spin degree of freedom in this insulating state of ultrathin SiNM may differ for different valleys, so that the SiNM can be exploited to produce the spatially separated spin and valley currents. We propose that the tunable spin-polarized valley photocurrents can be generated in an experimentally feasible ellipsometry setup. Our work shows promise for the development of spintronic and valleytronic devices compatible with current silicon industry.
