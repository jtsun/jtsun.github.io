Personal Profile

Dr. Jiatao Sun has graduated from department of materials sciences and engineering, Shandong University in 2002 and 2005 with BA and MS respectively. Then he moved to Institute of Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences (IOP, CAS) and graduated in 2008 with a Ph. D majored in condensed matter physics. Afterwards, he continued his science career with postdoctoral position. He has stayed in National University of Singapore and University of Utah (USA) for several years. In the end of 2013, he came back to Institute of Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences with a position of associate professor.

His research interest mainly lies at the theoretical investigation of quantum phenomena in low dimensional systems by using density functional theory (DFT) with model Hamiltonian. His past research experience includes the self-assembly mechanism of molecular adsorption on surfaces, design of electronic structures around surface and interface. These projects were done partially in collaboration with scanning tunneling microscopy experiments. Recently his main research interest is theoretical design of new quantum materials by using DFT calculations and Wannier functions including some physical questions such as the magnetism and quantum anomalous Hall effecton emerging at surface or interface, spintronics and valleytronics in two-dimensional materials etc. His main achievements include the discovery of spin-polarized state on pristine Si thin film, larger energy gap formed at the edge of Bi nanoribbon, change of Au(111) surface reconstruction etc. He has published about 40 papers in journals like PRL, Nano Lett., Adv. Mater., PRB, APL etc. The complete publication list is shown at ResearcherID.
